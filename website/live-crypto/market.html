Crypto

Cryptocurrency, often referred to as "crypto" for short, is a digital or virtual form of currency that uses cryptography for security. Unlike traditional currencies issued by governments (such as the US dollar or the euro), cryptocurrencies operate on decentralized networks based on blockchain technology.

Mining

Mining in the context of cryptocurrency refers to the process of validating and verifying transactions on a blockchain network and adding them to the blockchain's public ledger. This process is crucial for the security and functionality of many cryptocurrencies, including Bitcoin.

Blockchain

A blockchain is a distributed ledger technology that records transactions in a secure and transparent manner. It consists of a chain of blocks, each containing a list of transactions. Here are some key features of blockchain technology: Decentralization:
Blockchains are typically decentralized networks, meaning they are not controlled by a single central authority, such as a government or a bank. Instead, they are maintained by a network of nodes (computers) that validate and record transactions.
